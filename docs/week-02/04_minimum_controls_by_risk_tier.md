# Minimum Controls by Risk Tier

## Tier 1 — Assistive / Low Risk

### Minimum Required Controls
- Named AI Business Owner
- Basic usage logging
- Non-authoritative output disclaimer
- Data classification awareness

### Oversight Model
- End-user verification

---

## Tier 2 — Decision Support / Medium Risk

### Minimum Required Controls
- Mandatory Human-in-the-Loop (End-user or SME)
- Explainable or evidence-backed outputs
- Decision and override logging
- Defined confidence thresholds
- Security and data protection controls
- Vendor fallback or exit strategy

### Oversight Model
- End-user verification
- SME review for material decisions

---

## Tier 3 — Decision Execution / High Risk

### Minimum Required Controls (Mandatory)
- Executive Business Owner accountability
- Explicit Legal and Risk sign-off
- Independent AI Auditor involvement
- Strict Human-in-the-Loop enforcement
- Policy-bound conditional autonomy only
- Full audit logs and traceability
- Kill-switch and rollback mechanisms
- **Mandatory quarterly “Red-Button” kill-switch drills**
- Continuous monitoring and alerting
- Tested vendor fallback or manual override
- Defined re-certification cadence

### Oversight Model
- SME review for domain correctness
- Independent AI Auditor for systemic oversight

---

## Control Escalation Rule

Controls must **escalate with risk**, not convenience.  
Downgrading controls without formal re-evaluation constitutes a **governance breach**.
